Case Study: Digital Asset Management Software Research at Dataminr

Owner: Lizeth Anaya-Ojeda

Role: UX Researcher and Project Manager

_____________

Collaborators: Sarah Goodgoll

Role: Manager and Product Manager

Project Overview

Dataminr, a leading real-time information discovery platform, recognized the need to enhance its digital asset management capabilities. In response to this, a comprehensive research project was initiated to identify, evaluate, and procure a Digital Asset Management (DAM) software solution that aligned with the company's unique business needs.

Project Objectives

  1. Identify and assess DAM software vendors in the market.
  2. Facilitate meetings with vendors to understand their offerings and capabilities.
  3. Create a structured list of questions for vendors based on stakeholder input to evaluate vendors.
  4. Develop a comprehensive chart comparing vendor features.
  5. Articulate a compelling business case justifying the need for DAM software.
  6. Collaborate with management to create a budget proposal for software procurement.

Project Timeline

The project was executed over a span of four months, from January to April.

January: Needs Assessment and Stakeholder Meetings

  • Conducted interviews and meetings with internal stakeholders (marketing, content, and IT teams) to fully understand the organization’s DAM requirements.
  • Identified key pain points in current asset management processes, such as inefficiencies in file search and version control.
  • Defined selection criteria for the DAM solution, prioritizing scalability, user-friendliness, and integration capabilities with existing tools.

Lizeth: This phase marked a significant shift in my approach to research. I quickly recognized the need to actively listen for underlying challenges that weren’t immediately apparent in initial conversations. By focusing on understanding stakeholders’ specific workflows and frustrations, I refined my ability to ask more nuanced questions. I also learned the importance of managing conflicting needs among stakeholders, which strengthened my coordination skills and set the stage for clear, informed decision-making as the project progressed.

February: Vendor Identification and Outreach

  • Researched potential DAM software vendors, narrowing down options to Bynder, Canto, and Brandfolder based on specific criteria from stakeholder interviews.
  • Initiated outreach to vendors, organizing introductory meetings to assess their offerings and compatibility with Dataminr’s needs.
  • Collaborated with stakeholders to refine evaluation criteria for vendor meetings, ensuring the focus was on integration ease, user experience, and support.

Lizeth:This phase challenged me to refine my research process. I developed a more systematic approach to evaluating vendors, relying on both objective data and qualitative insights from stakeholders. I found myself balancing technical specifications with the softer aspects of vendor relationships, such as communication style and responsiveness. Managing initial outreach was particularly instructive in establishing a clear, professional tone that fostered productive early-stage conversations, and I gained confidence in navigating vendor negotiations and building rapport.

March: Questionnaire Development and Vendor Meetings

  • Worked with stakeholders to develop a detailed questionnaire for shortlisted vendors, focusing on key features like metadata management, search functionality, and customization options.
  • Facilitated in-depth meetings with Bynder, Canto, and Brandfolder to evaluate their solutions. Documented responses and insights on their strengths, weaknesses, and unique offerings.
  • Synthesized feedback from vendor meetings into a comprehensive comparison report to guide decision-making.

Lizeth: In this phase, I refined my ability to translate abstract needs into precise, actionable questions. Collaborating with stakeholders on the questionnaire forced me to synthesize both their technical requirements and business priorities into a coherent set of evaluation criteria. I also grew more effective in managing vendor meetings, ensuring that I collected the necessary details while staying on track. The documentation of insights became a crucial skill, as I learned to organize complex vendor responses into comparative, decision-enabling formats.

April: Feature Comparison and Analysis

  • Compiled data from vendor meetings into a feature comparison chart, clearly displaying the capabilities and limitations of each software solution.
  • Collaborated with cross-functional teams to evaluate and prioritize features, considering business goals such as streamlining workflows and improving asset search.
  • Presented the feature chart to decision-makers, gathering feedback to refine the selection process and ensure alignment with business needs.
  • Developed a detailed business case based on stakeholder input and vendor insights, emphasizing the potential for enhanced productivity, better collaboration, and improved asset tracking.

Lizeth:This phase required me to integrate extensive research into a clear, actionable narrative. By synthesizing detailed vendor responses into a concise comparison, I honed my ability to prioritize features based on business needs, making complex information more accessible for decision-makers. I also strengthened my skills in aligning technical solutions with broader organizational objectives, framing the business case in terms of productivity gains and operational efficiencies. This process sharpened my ability to present data strategically and refine project outcomes based on stakeholder feedback.

Results

🏆 Secured Budget & Vendor Partnership: I led the successful budget proposal, ensuring necessary funding for the DAM software acquisition, and managed the partnership with the vendor, laying the groundwork for a seamless future implementation.

🏆 Addressed Key Pain Points through Research & Strategy: As the research lead, I identified and defined core pain points, such as inefficient asset retrieval and inconsistent branding, and strategically selected the DAM software to address these issues, improving workflow efficiency and brand consistency.

🏆 Strategic Planning for Future Efficiency & Growth: I developed a comprehensive plan for the integration of advanced search, version control, and automated tagging, positioning Dataminr for improved asset management and data-driven growth once the DAM system is fully implemented.
